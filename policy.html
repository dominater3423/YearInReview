Notes on American Public Policy

There, I believe, are ten lectures in total. I'm only going to put here the limited notes I took for the two covering regulation and immigration.

Regulation

  • policy is made by administrative agencies
  • neither courts nor administrative agencies are elected and aren't accountable the way they're supposed to be
  • the department of justice is the agency that put in motion the changes in immigration policy
  • study of the law that governs these agencies needs to be paid attention to
  • limited government allows us to employ our own devices
  • all ideologies want to impose a doctrine and way of life

Immigration

  • very little clear thinking of mass immigration on the character of America
  • if all men are created equal then government must be based on the consent of the governed
  • if born in a particular place then you're a citizen of said place
  • you are tied to the land of your birth
  • birth right citizenship is a throwback to feudalism
  • citizenship is a social contract
  • according to the constitution a citizen if born in the United States and subject to its jurisdiction
  • children follow descent of parents, not the child
  • people have the right to leave
  • birthright citizenship was focused on freeing the slaves
  • supreme court threw out consent of citizenship
  • people coming to the United States can claim that under your laws there's nothing you can do about me coming here
  • no national right to citizenship
  • more duties which exist between citizens composes the law under these principles
  • non-citizens have no rights
  • republican governments require a particular kind of citizen
  • if one wants to retain rights one must fight to defend them
  • people's governments conform certain principles on them, and somebody who has come from an alternative form of government will have a tough time with integration
  • new citizens have to adopt principles of government
  • problem when too many immigrants form in one place because they retain their native culture
  • America has a right and duty to rationalize the immigration process in favor of the American people themselves, not foreigners
